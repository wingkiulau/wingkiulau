History and GIS Project: A comparison of Northwest Georgia from 1830-1850 preceding and following the Cherokee Removal of 1838 using GIS analysis.

Complementing my double major in Computer Science and History, one of my favorite projects was a cross-departmental study in History and Digital Computational Studies on post-Trail of Tears and Georgia Gold Rush development in Northwest Georgia, ending before the Civil War in 1861. This project used GIS analysis to examine how Georgiaâ€™s industry and infrastructure developed on former Cherokee lands from 1830-1850 by comparing Cherokee settlements and territorial boundaries with corresponding Georgia counties. I researched the historical background using academic journals and books and analyzed demographic, economic, and infrastructural data from the NHGIS database. Utilizing ArcGIS and Excel for data analysis and visualization, I created maps to extract meaningful insights. This project highlighted my passion for working with data and history.
